ABSTRACT


There have been many recent developments in 3D display technology to provide correct focus cues. Multi-plane displays, which are promising because they can in principle provide those cues with conventional display hardware, support a number of focal planes at a cost : frame rate, eye-box, or spatial resolution. Tomographic displays may improve upon these tradeoffs, but they are suffering from difficulties in supporting the optimal presentation of backlight pattern and display panel due to its intrinsic binary constraints. In this work, we present a technique for displaying three-dimensional imagery of general scenes on tomographic displays. Requiring no prior knowledge of the scene geometry, our technique addresses the blending issue via non-convex optimization. Also, we present a general framework for this rendering algorithm and demonstrate the utility of the technique for a diverse system which uses binary data.




Figure 1. Schematic diagram to describe the principle of tomographic displays that employ a focus-tunable lens. As shown in the figure, FSAB(Fast Spatially Adjustable Backlight) and focus-tunable lens are synchronized to provide users with the depth information. When the image of the display panel is formed at the depth of z1, the FSAB illuminates selectively blue dice of the display panel. When the focus-tunable lens forms the display panel image at the depth of z2, the FSAB illuminates selectively red dice of the display panel. As a result, blue and red dice seem to be floated at the depth of z1 and z2, respectively. Note that each depth can be negative value, which means the image can be created at the left-side of the focus-tunable lens as well.
