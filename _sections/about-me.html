I am a first-year MSc student at Stanford University EE department and supported by Kwanjeong Scholarship and Korea Government Scholarship. My research interests include computational displays/imaging, optimization techniques for optical systems, and light transport.

I was born in Seoul, Korea in 1994. I was a silver medalist at the IPhO (International Physics Olympiad) 2012. As graduating from Seoul Science High School in 2013, I entered Seoul National University majoring in Electrical and Computer Engineering. I was a recipient of Presidential Science Scholarship for undergraduate study.

From Aug 2015 to May 2017, I served in the Crime Prevention Police Company at Seoul Eunpyeong Police Station and the Korean National Police Agency Cyber Bureau as a substitute for military service. There, I worked as a full-stack software engineer and managed the national performance management system.

During my undergraduate, I was advised by Profs. Deog-Kyoon Jeong, Soo-Mook Moon, and Byoungho Lee.

Here at Stanford, I am fortunate to work with Prof. Gordon Wetzstein.
